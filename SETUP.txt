  ║   Radio Alarm — Setup Guide          ║
  ╚══════════════════════════════════════╝


  WHAT YOU NEED
  ─────────────
  - Raspberry Pi Zero 2 W (you ordered this)
  - 32GB SD card with Pi OS (you ordered this)
  - USB-C cable + any phone charger
  - Your WiFi name and password


  STEP 1: Prepare the SD card
  ───────────────────────────
  Before putting the SD card in the Pi, plug it into your Mac.

  Open "Raspberry Pi Imager" (download from raspberrypi.com/software
  if you don't have it). Even though your SD card is preloaded,
  the Imager lets you pre-configure WiFi — which is much easier
  than connecting a monitor.

  In the Imager:
    • Choose OS → "Raspberry Pi OS Lite (32-bit)"
    • Choose Storage → your SD card
    • Click the gear icon (⚙) and fill in:
        - Hostname: raspberrypi
        - Enable SSH: yes (use password)
        - Username: pi
        - Password: (pick something simple)
        - WiFi SSID: your WiFi name
        - WiFi Password: your WiFi password
        - Country: US
    • Click Write

  This takes ~5 minutes.


  STEP 2: Boot the Pi
  ────────────────────
  Put the SD card in the Pi. Plug it into power.
  Wait 2 minutes for it to boot and connect to WiFi.


  STEP 3: Copy the alarm files to the Pi
  ───────────────────────────────────────
  Open Terminal on your Mac and run:

    scp -r /path/to/wkcr-alarm pi@raspberrypi.local:~/alarm

  (Replace /path/to/wkcr-alarm with wherever this folder
  is on your Mac. You can drag the folder into Terminal
  to paste its path.)

  Type "yes" if it asks about fingerprints, then enter
  the password you chose in Step 1.


  STEP 4: Install everything
  ──────────────────────────
  Still in Terminal:

    ssh pi@raspberrypi.local
    (enter your password)

    cd ~/alarm
    bash install.sh

  This takes ~3 minutes. It installs everything automatically.


  STEP 5: Open the settings page
  ───────────────────────────────
  On your phone or laptop, go to:

    http://raspberrypi.local:8550

  Bookmark this. That's your alarm control page.

  Pick your speaker, choose a station, set the time, save.
  Done!


  OPTIONAL: Access from anywhere (Tailscale)
  ───────────────────────────────────────────
  The install script already put Tailscale on your Pi.
  To finish setting it up:

  1. SSH into the Pi:
       ssh pi@raspberrypi.local

  2. Run:
       sudo tailscale up

  3. It prints a link — open it in your browser and sign in
     with Google (or whatever you prefer).

  4. Install Tailscale on your phone too:
       - iPhone: App Store → "Tailscale"
       - Android: Play Store → "Tailscale"
       Sign in with the same account.

  5. Now find your Pi's Tailscale IP:
       tailscale ip -4

     It'll be something like 100.64.x.x

  6. On your phone, go to:
       http://100.64.x.x:8550

     This works from anywhere — coffee shop, work, vacation.
     Share the link with your partner so they can control it too.
     (They just need Tailscale installed + same account, or
     you can share the device with their account.)


  TROUBLESHOOTING
  ───────────────
  "raspberrypi.local doesn't work"
    → Find the Pi's IP on your router's admin page
      (usually 192.168.1.x). Use that instead.

  "No speakers found"
    → Make sure the Pi and Google Home are on the same WiFi.

  "Alarm didn't go off"
    → SSH in and check: sudo systemctl status radio-alarm

  To restart the alarm service:
    ssh pi@raspberrypi.local
    sudo systemctl restart radio-alarm
